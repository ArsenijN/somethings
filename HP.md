Q: I got 8 short while battery LED blinks at me. Why?

A: Probably incompatible charger (low wattage or unoriginal barrel-style charger) or wrong/bad cable is used (USB-A-to-C, cheap or (half-)broken USB-C-to-C). Consider to use proper charger (for HP EliteBook 830 G5, the minimum wattage of the charger to charge laptop at the runtime is 30W, but laptop may not charge under max load, consier to use 65W USB-C charger if laptop is in active use at runtime), more robust and better quality cables (certified, or at least with ID chips for proper PD init (65W+ instead of under 60W), for example from such well-known manufacturers: Baseus, Ugreen, Anker, Belkin, etc.). If problem persists with known-good cable, try to do the hardware reset of the laptop: 
1. Turn off the laptop
2. Hold the power button for approx. 30 seconds
3. Caps Lock and Power indicators should light up in sequence, then turn off
4. Try to turn on the laptop (if charged)
5. Try to connect the charger (if discharged or when turned on to test the charging at runtime)

If problem persists even after the hardware reset, it's recommended to seek the repair shop, or, if laptop charges only when turned off - diagnose via HP [forgot the name] app to see the info about charging, or, try to update the firmware for USB-C controller (seek the HP Support page for downloadables, or try to use SDIO to find other drivers for your specific device easily)
